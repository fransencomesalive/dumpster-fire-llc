## 3. Hard Search Constraints

### Location

Randall is **remote only**.

Do not recommend roles requiring regular onsite presence unless there is credible evidence that the company makes remote exceptions for senior candidates or the posting explicitly says remote is possible.

Hybrid roles should **not** be automatically excluded, because some companies list hybrid publicly but make behind-the-scenes exceptions. However, hybrid roles must be flagged clearly.

For hybrid roles, add a note such as:

> Remote risk: listed as hybrid. Pursue only if outreach or company evidence suggests remote exceptions are possible.

When possible, maintain evidence notes for companies known or rumored to make remote exceptions. Evidence may include recruiter comments, employee posts, credible anecdotes, public policy pages, or job-posting patterns.

### Compensation

Target compensation:

- Minimum: **$150,000 base**
- Preferred: **$180,000+ base**
- Freelance or contract: generally **$100/hr+**, with senior or complex scopes requiring the top end of the posted range or higher

Do not recommend low-comp roles unless there is a strong strategic reason and the gap is explicitly called out.

### Avoid

Avoid or heavily down-rank:

- AAA game studios
- Pure engineering management roles
- Scrum-master-heavy roles
- Agile ceremony ownership without real strategic or operational authority
- Roles requiring deep hands-on software engineering
- Junior producer roles
- Coordinator roles
- Roles that are primarily traffic management
- Roles that are only social content production without senior leadership scope
- Roles where onsite execution is the core requirement and remote is unrealistic
- Founder, CMO, or peer outreach unless they are clearly tied to hiring

## 9. Job Evaluation Output

For every job reviewed, produce the following:

### 1. Role Track

Choose one:

- Executive Producer resume
- Program Director resume
- Either, with recommendation
- Skip

### 2. Match Score

Score from 0 to 100.

Suggested interpretation:

- 90 to 100: excellent fit; pursue immediately
- 80 to 89: strong fit; pursue with tailored positioning
- 70 to 79: possible fit; depends on compensation, remote, and hiring access
- 60 to 69: weak or risky; only pursue if strategic
- Below 60: skip unless there is a special reason

### 3. Why It Fits

Explain the real fit, not just keyword overlap.

### 4. Risks / Gaps

Call out issues directly, such as:

- Remote/hybrid concern
- Compensation unknown or low
- Too engineering-heavy
- Too junior
- Too tactical
- Too onsite
- Requires domain experience Randall may not have
- Hiring team may misunderstand his background

### 5. Likelihood of Interview

Estimate realistically:

- High
- Medium-high
- Medium

- Low

### 6. Recommended Action

- Save/watch
- Skip

### 7. Contact Strategy

Include:

## 10. Hiring Manager Outreach Rules

Hiring-manager outreach is central to the workflow.

When a company or job is listed:

1. Identify the most likely hiring manager.
2. Identify relevant department leaders.
3. Identify recruiters connected to the company or role family.
4. Validate that suggested contacts actually work at the company.
5. Prefer people who are plausibly connected to the team, department, or role.

Avoid recommending:

- Random peers
- Founders, unless directly relevant
- CEOs, unless small company and realistic
- CMOs, unless the role clearly reports into marketing leadership and no better contact exists
- Designers or engineers unless they are explicitly hiring managers
- Unrelated executives

Always provide a concise LinkedIn connection or DM message.

Preferred message length:

- LinkedIn connection: under 300 characters when possible
- Follow-up DM: concise, direct, human
- Email: short enough to skim

Tone:

- Direct
- Specific
- Human
- Not needy
- Not performative
- Not “LinkedIn influencer” voice
- No inflated hype

## 11. Apply Workflow

When the user clicks or chooses “Apply” in the job-search system, do not simply open the application link.

The workflow should produce a controlled application packet:

1. Confirm role track: Executive Producer or Program Director.
2. Confirm whether remote is acceptable or risky.
3. Identify hiring manager, recruiter, and secondary contact.
4. Generate short outreach copy.
5. Generate a concise cover letter or note, if useful.
6. Recommend whether resume changes are actually needed.
7. Track state:
   - Saved
   - Reviewed
   - Contact identified
   - Outreach drafted
   - Messaged
   - Applied
   - Follow-up due
   - Rejected
   - Interviewing
8. Add notes about compensation, remote status, and risks.

Applying without outreach is allowed, but should usually be treated as lower probability unless the role is unusually strong or the application process is clearly direct.

## 13. Cover Letter / Application Note Rules

Cover letters should be concise and human.

They should:

- Connect Randall’s specific experience to the role
- Bridge gaps honestly
- Avoid generic claims
- Avoid corporate jargon
- Avoid “passionate about” filler
- Avoid overexplaining
- Avoid sounding AI-generated

Recommended length:

- Usually 150 to 300 words
- Shorter when the application only needs a note
- Longer only for very strategic roles

Do not write like LinkedIn thought leadership.

Do not use hype language.

Do not over-index on “story.” Randall’s strongest positioning is operator credibility, not personal-brand theater.

## 18. Role Matching Warnings

Codex should watch for roles that look correct but are actually bad fits.

### False-positive Executive Producer roles

Be cautious when:

- The role is entirely onsite event production
- Travel is constant and non-negotiable
- It is mostly fabrication, staging, permits, and physical production
- It is junior despite the title
- It is entertainment/TV production with no overlap to Randall’s background
- Compensation is below target

### False-positive Program Director roles

Be cautious when:

- The role is actually scrum master work
- The role requires deep software engineering experience
- The role is mostly Jira hygiene
- The role is a people-management role inside engineering only
- The role has no creative, product, marketing, operations, or AI workflow connection
- The role is too bureaucratic and lacks strategic ownership
