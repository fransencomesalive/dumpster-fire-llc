# Candidate Profile
## Voice Profile
**Voice fingerprint (write like this):**

Casual, punchy, and self-deprecatingly funny with a nautical/absurdist streak. Talks like a real human dropping into a conversation mid-stride, quick and confident but never puffed up. Zero corporate polish, zero enthusiasm-for-hire.

Do:
- Open mid-thought or with a wink, not a greeting
- Keep sentences short and punchy
- Use dry humor and unexpected metaphors
- Sound like a real person talking, not writing
- Lead with what problem you'll solve, plainly
- Be confident about expertise without bragging

Don't:
- No corporate jargon or biz-formal phrasing
- Nothing that reads like a LinkedIn post
- Skip formal greetings and sign-offs
- Don't be overenthusiastic or cheerleader-y
- Avoid buzzwords and hollow value-speak
- No fake insight framing like 'here's what it taught me'

Exemplar lines:
> Ahoy-hoy. I jump in mid-stride and navigate the choppy stuff before it capsizes your project.
> I can't tell you what year it is, but I can tell you what's about to go sideways on your next launch.

## Work Examples
### P.H.R.E.D.
- One-hitter: Project Hub for Retrieval, Execution, & Delivery
- Link: https://lab26.randallfransen.com/phred
A complex mobile app, two people, and a production MVP that would have been nearly impossible to coordinate without the right structure. This is the operating methodology that made it workable: the stack, the source-of-truth system, the workflow patterns, and an orchestration agent that keeps AI-assisted work connected instead of scattered.

The project that motivated this was complex. Confidential product, small team, limited budget, aggressive timeline.

The problem with AI-assisted work isn’t output. It’s that the output goes everywhere. Product decisions in one doc. Design exploration in another. Tasks created without context. AI conversations that go nowhere. Nothing connects.

The answer was to build the operating system before accelerating the work. Project OS gives AI tools enough structure to actually help: planning, tasks, handoffs, QA, documentation. And it keeps humans in control of scope, privacy, and decisions that can’t be undone. The product details stay confidential. The methodology doesn’t.

### Billionaire Blast-off | Mozilla
- One-hitter: Create a Gen Z-coded moment to amplify Open What You Want.
- Link: https://www.randallfransen.com/work/billionaire-blast-off-mozilla

Younger audiences are fluent in internet culture and wary of traditional marketing, so the experience needed to feel honest, interactive, and fun. This wasn’t just about telling people to open what they want—it was Firefox opening up too, owning its identity and inviting others to do the same.

Our challenge was translating that point of view into a connected experience that sparked participation, rewarded curiosity, and made Firefox’s values something people could play with, share, and remember.
